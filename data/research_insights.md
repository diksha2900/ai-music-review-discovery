# Research Questions — Direct Answers

### Why do users struggle to discover new music?

Users struggle to discover new music due to issues with Spotify's recommendation algorithm. Across both Twitter and Play Store sources, users express frustration with the algorithm, using strong language to describe it as "stupid worthless", "shittiest algo", and calling for it to be "taken out back". Many users feel that the recommendations are not effective, with one user stating that "half the songs I've already heard" and another saying that the algorithm "always showing me" the same things. Some users also feel that the algorithm is "so clunky" and has become "worse" over time. Despite this, some users do appreciate Spotify's playlists, such as "Discover Weekly" and "On Repeat playlists", suggesting that there are some effective tools for music discovery. However, the overwhelming sentiment is that the algorithm needs improvement, with users explicitly stating "fix ur recommendations" and "I need new music". Overall, the quotes suggest that users are not satisfied with the current state of music discovery on Spotify.

### What are the most common frustrations with recommendations?

The most common frustrations with recommendations are centered around repetition and a lack of diversity. Users express frustration with hearing the "same songs" and "same sounding songs on repeat", indicating that the algorithm is not providing enough variety. This sentiment is echoed across sources, with users on Twitter and the Play Store describing the recommendations as "repetitive" and stating that they "hear the same song" repeatedly. Some users also feel that the algorithm is "stuck" and unable to provide new or different music, with one user stating that they are "stuck in the 200M range". The use of strong language, such as "worst recommendations" and "stupid worthless algorithm", further emphasizes the strength of these frustrations. Overall, the data suggests that users are seeking more diverse and dynamic recommendations from Spotify. Users are explicitly asking for improvement, with one user saying "fix ur recommendations".

### What listening behaviors are users trying to achieve?

Users are trying to achieve music discovery and exploration through their listening behaviors. This is evident from quotes such as "I need new music", "find new music", and "music exploration", which suggest a desire to discover new artists or songs. The mention of specific playlists like "Discover Weekly" and "Rediscover Weekly playlist" also supports this idea. Additionally, quotes like "made me listen" and "auto play recommended" imply that users appreciate features that introduce them to new content. The desire for discovery is further emphasized by phrases like "find the next", indicating a desire to continue exploring new music. Across sources, users value the ability to easily find and listen to new music, with one user even mentioning "my favourite people", suggesting a desire to discover new music from their favorite artists. Overall, users are seeking a seamless and engaging music exploration experience.

### What causes users to repeatedly listen to the same content?

Users repeatedly listen to the same content on Spotify due to a combination of factors, including the algorithm's recommendations and playlist features. Across both Twitter and Play Store comments, users express frustration with repetitive song suggestions, with phrases like "same songs", "same sounding songs on repeat", and "same three songs" indicating a sense of monotony. Some users appreciate specific playlists, such as "topnotch playlists" and "On Repeat playlists", which may contribute to repeated listening. However, others criticize the algorithm, using strong language like "stupid worthless algorithm" and "shittiest algo", suggesting that the recommendations are not meeting their expectations. The repetition is also attributed to features like auto-play and the Discover Weekly playlist, which can lead to a "de ja vu" experience. Overall, the data suggests that users are both positively and negatively influenced by Spotify's algorithm and playlist features, leading to repeated listening of the same content. Users also mention that they "love Spotify" despite these issues, indicating a strong loyalty to the platform.

### Which user segments experience different discovery challenges?

Based on the user quotes, two distinct user segments can be identified: Power Listeners and Casual Listeners. Power Listeners, who use features like Discover Weekly and Rediscover Weekly, experience frustration with repetitive and same-sounding song recommendations, as evident from quotes like "same sounding songs on repeat" and "worst recommendations". In contrast, Casual Listeners seem to enjoy the ease of use and the variety of music available, with quotes like "easy to use" and "most worldwide music". Another segment, Genre-Specific Listeners, is hinted at through quotes like "gives me all my gospel", suggesting that users with specific musical tastes face challenges in discovering new music within their preferred genre. Additionally, a segment of users who value discovery and exploration, as opposed to those who prefer familiar music, is implied by quotes like "made me listen" and "hits the Discover Weekly", highlighting their unique challenge of finding new and interesting content. Across sources, a common thread of dissatisfaction with recommendation algorithms is evident, with quotes like "shittiest algo" and "レコメンドのロジック", suggesting that users across segments face challenges with music discovery.

### What unmet needs emerge consistently across reviews?

Across reviews from Twitter and the Play Store, a consistent unmet need emerges: dissatisfaction with Spotify's music recommendations. Users express frustration with repetitive and same-sounding songs, with quotes such as "same sounding songs on repeat", "same music every day", and "same three songs" highlighting this issue. Some users also mention that the recommendations are not effective, with phrases like "not recommendations", "worst recommendations", and "shittiest algo" indicating a strong negative sentiment. Additionally, users mention that they often hear the same songs or artists repeatedly, with quotes like "plays songs like the whole artist's" and "get a repeat track" supporting this claim. The dissatisfaction with recommendations is not limited to one source, as similar complaints are found in both Twitter and Play Store reviews, such as "déçu de mes Discover Weekly" from an unspecified source and "Rediscover Weekly playlist" which may imply a desire for improvement. Overall, the data suggests that users are seeking more diverse and effective music recommendations from Spotify. Users are looking for a better discovery experience, with some even expressing love for the platform but disappointment with its current recommendation capabilities.

# Findings by Theme

## Recommendation Quality (21 records)

Users are dissatisfied with the recommendation quality, citing repetitive and irrelevant songs. The root cause appears to be an ineffective algorithm. Users are expressing frustration, with one saying "worst recommendations". This frustration leads to negative reviews and potential churn. Users expect personalized and diverse playlists, but the current algorithm falls short. The algorithm's inability to adapt to user preferences is a major concern.

## Repetition Fatigue (12 records)

The root cause of the issue appears to be the algorithm's inability to provide diverse music recommendations. Users are exhibiting frustration and disappointment with the repetitive content, leading to a decrease in engagement. As one user noted, "same songs" are being played repeatedly. This repetition fatigue is causing users to seek alternative music platforms. Users are looking for a more varied and dynamic music experience, but the current algorithm is failing to deliver. The lack of diversity is leading to a stale user experience.

## Discovery Effort (12 records)

The root cause of user frustration is the difficulty in finding new music. Users often rely on discovery features to find new content. As one user noted, "I need new music". This behavior indicates that users are actively seeking new experiences. Users expect the platform to facilitate music exploration and discovery. The platform's ability to recommend new music is crucial to user satisfaction.

## Algorithm Trust/Distrust (5 records)

Users are expressing distrust in algorithms, particularly with the integration of AI. The root cause of this distrust is the perceived lack of transparency and control. As a result, users are becoming increasingly skeptical of recommendations. A user simply states "ai slop", reflecting their dissatisfaction. This sentiment is likely to impact user behavior, leading to decreased engagement and potential abandonment of platforms. Users are seeking more authentic experiences, unmediated by AI influences.

## Social/Identity Signaling (1 records)

The root cause of this behavior is the desire for social connection and identity formation. Users often express their preferences to signal their identity and values. This is evident in user behavior, where they openly state their likes and dislikes. For instance, a user simply says "my favourite people". This statement showcases how users utilize preferences to convey their social identity. Users' statements like this can be seen as a form of social signaling, revealing their personality and values.
